# Lesson Plan- (Pre K - K): Birth of Jesus

**Date:** Sunday, December 6, 2009

**Title:** Lesson Plan- (Pre K - K): Birth of Jesus

**Objectives:** The children will be able to

- retell the story of Jesus’ birth
- identify that Jesus was born in Bethlehem
- identify that Mary and Joseph went to Bethlehem because of the census
- identify that Jesus was born in a stable because there was no room at the inn
- identify that the shepherds were the first to visit Jesus
- identify that the angels announced Jesus’ birth to the shepherds

**Plan:**

### Circle Time:

Read story: “The Birth of Jesus” from your own story book. Then ask questions from below.

- Where was Jesus born? (Bethlehem)  
- Why did Mary and Joseph go to Bethlehem? (Because of the census)  
- Why was Jesus born in a stable? (There was no room at the inn)  
- Who were the first to visit Jesus? (The shepherds)  
- Who announced Jesus’ birth to the shepherds? (Angels)  
- What did the angels say? (Glory to God in the highest and on earth peace to those on whom his favor rests.)  
- What did the shepherds do after the angels left? (They went to Bethlehem to see Jesus.)  
- What did the shepherds do after they saw Jesus? (They spread the word concerning what had been told them about this child.)  

**Discussion Questions:**  

- Why do you think God chose to have Jesus born in a stable? (To show that Jesus came for all people, not just the rich and powerful.)  
- What can we learn from the shepherds? (To be open to God’s message, to share the good news.)

**Away in a Manger Finger Play:**  
(Do actions as you sing the song.)  
Away in a manger, no crib for a bed, (rock baby in arms)  
The little Lord Jesus laid down his sweet head. (lay head on hands)  
The stars in the bright sky looked down where he lay, (look up)  
The little Lord Jesus asleep on the hay. (close eyes and pretend to sleep)  
The cattle are lowing, the baby awakes, (cow sound)  
But little Lord Jesus no crying he makes. (shake head no)  
I love thee, Lord Jesus! Look down from the sky, (look up and wave)  
And stay by my side until morning is nigh. (hold arms out)
